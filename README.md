# Fuel Good

**Eat clean. Earn your cheat meals. Feel the difference.**

---

## The Problem

Most people know they should eat better. But the nutrition industry has made healthy eating feel like punishment — calorie counting, restriction, guilt, and boring food. The result? People swing between unsustainable diets and giving up entirely.

Meanwhile, the real cost of poor nutrition compounds silently: brain fog, low energy, weight that won't budge, metabolic dysfunction, and a general feeling of "off" that people accept as normal. Not because they lack willpower — but because they lack a system that works *with* human nature instead of against it.

## The Insight

**Your body cares more about the presence of the good than the absence of the bad.**

You don't need to be perfect. You don't need to eliminate every indulgence. What matters is that most of the time, you're fueling your body with real, whole foods that give it what it needs — quality protein, fiber, micronutrients, and minimal processed junk.

When you do that consistently, something shifts. You feel it. More energy. Sharper thinking. Better sleep. And here's the part nobody talks about: when your baseline nutrition is strong, your body handles the occasional pizza night, dessert, or takeout without missing a beat.

**Eating healthy and eating *well* are not mutually exclusive.** The meals in Fuel Good prove it — they're designed to be genuinely delicious, easy to make, and nutritionally excellent. This isn't salads and sadness. This is Chicken Shawarma Bowls, Homestyle Smash Burgers, and Turkish Eggs with Whipped Feta.

## The Philosophy

Fuel Good is built on a reward-based system, not a restriction-based one:

1. **Eat clean, tasty whole-food meals** throughout the week — following your plan or logging your own
2. **Watch your Fuel Score rise** as your food quality improves
3. **Earn Flex Meals** — guilt-free cheat meals you've genuinely earned through consistency
4. **Feel the difference** in your energy, focus, and how your body responds

The psychology is simple: instead of "I can't have that," it becomes "I'm earning that." Every clean meal is a step toward your next flex meal. Every flex meal is a reward you deserve because you put in the work.

## The Scoring System

Fuel Good uses two complementary scores that measure different things. Together they give users a complete picture of their nutrition — one measures *what* you're eating, the other measures *how well* it fuels your body.

### Fuel Score — "Is This Real Food?"

A 0-100 score that measures ingredient quality: how whole, unprocessed, and clean the food is. Not calories, not macros — *ingredient integrity*. The Fuel Score answers one question: **are you eating real food or processed substitutes?**

**Every curated Fuel Good recipe scores 100.** They're designed with whole food ingredients by definition — no seed oils, no refined flour, no artificial preservatives, no added sugars. When you eat from the app, you're guaranteed perfect fuel.

The score drops when processing enters the picture:

| Score | What it means | Examples |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **100** | All whole food ingredients, zero processing | Fuel Good recipes, home-cooked from scratch with whole ingredients |
| **80-99** | Mostly whole, minor processed elements | Home meal with store-bought tortilla, bottled sauce, or pre-made seasoning |
| **60-79** | Mix of whole and processed | Restaurant meal with real ingredients but some processed components |
| **40-59** | Significantly processed | Fast casual with some fresh elements (Chipotle-level) |
| **20-39** | Mostly processed | Fast food, packaged meals, heavy sauces, fried foods |
| **<20** | Ultra-processed | Candy, soda, frozen dinners, deep-fried fast food |

**What the Fuel Score detects:**
- Seed oils (canola, soybean, vegetable oil)
- Refined flour and grains
- Added sugars and artificial sweeteners
- Preservatives, emulsifiers, and artificial additives
- Processing level of each ingredient
- Whole food percentage of the overall meal

**Fuel Score tiers for weekly averages:**

| Tier | Weekly Avg | What it means |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Elite** | 90+ | Whole-food excellence — body is thriving |
| **Strong** | 75-89 | Consistently clean — flex meals fully absorbed |
| **Decent** | 60-74 | Good foundation, room for more whole foods |
| **Mixed** | 40-59 | Too much processed — reduce and earn flex back |
| **Flex** | <40 | Heavy processing — time to reset with clean meals |

### MES (Metabolic Energy Score) — "Will This Meal Give Me Energy?"

A separate 0-100 score that predicts how a meal affects your energy levels. While the Fuel Score measures ingredient quality, MES measures **metabolic balance** — does this meal have the right composition to sustain energy, or will it cause a crash?

Built on three personalized guardrails:

- **Protein target** — enough to maintain muscle, control hunger, and sustain energy
- **Fiber floor** — enough for gut health, blood sugar stability, and steady digestion
- **Sugar ceiling** — staying within your body's metabolic tolerance to prevent crashes

**Why these are separate scores:**

A fruit-heavy smoothie with oats and honey could be 100% whole food (**Fuel 100**) but metabolically suboptimal (**MES 55**) — high sugar, low protein, energy crash likely. Conversely, a protein bar with some processed ingredients might score **Fuel 60** but **MES 80** because the macro balance is solid.

Curated Fuel Good meals are **always Fuel 100** but **MES varies (typically 70-95)** depending on the meal's composition. This is intentional — it gives users two complementary signals:

- **Fuel Score**: "Am I eating real food?" → earns flex meals
- **MES**: "Am I eating balanced food?" → predicts how I'll feel

MES is personalized to your body — age, weight, activity level, metabolic health context (insulin resistance, blood sugar sensitivity, goals) all shape your individual targets.

## The Flex System — How Rewards Work

The flex system is the motivational engine of Fuel Good. It turns healthy eating from obligation into investment — every clean meal earns progress toward guilt-free indulgences.

### The Math That Proves the Philosophy

A typical week has 21 meals (3 per day). If a user follows their Fuel Good plan for most of those:

```
17 clean meals × 100 Fuel Score  = 1,700
 4 flex meals  ×  35 Fuel Score  =   140
─────────────────────────────────────────
Weekly average: 1,840 / 21       =  87.6  → Strong tier
```

**Even with 4 cheat meals, the weekly average stays at 87.6** — well above the 80 target. The 17 clean meals create such a strong baseline that the body absorbs the flex meals without negative impact. This is "presence of the good" in action.

### Why Flex Meals Count Toward the Average

Flex meals are **not** excluded from the weekly average. They're included because the average staying high *with* flex meals is the most powerful proof of the philosophy. Users see:

> "Weekly avg: 87 — even with 4 flex meals, your body is well-fueled"

This is the emotional permission users need. Not "you're allowed to cheat" but "the math proves your body handles this because you earned it."

### How Flex Meals Are Earned

The flex budget resets weekly. Users earn flex meals by maintaining their Fuel Score above their target (default 80) across enough meals:

- Eat whole-food meals consistently → Fuel Score stays high
- Weekly average above target → flex meals unlock progressively
- Each flex meal can be used guilt-free on anything — pizza, dessert, takeout, whatever
- The weekly average absorbs flex meals without dropping below the target threshold

### The Flex Budget Visualization

Users see their flex budget as a row of ticket icons that fill up as they earn them through clean eating. Each earned ticket represents a guilt-free indulgence. The visual is tangible and satisfying — like filling up a punch card where the reward is freedom to enjoy food you love.

## The Knowledge Gap — Why Scanning Changes Everything

Most people don't fail at healthy eating because they lack willpower. They fail because they genuinely don't know what's whole food and what isn't — especially for packaged items. The grocery store is overwhelming:

- Is "natural flavors" a real ingredient or a chemical umbrella term?
- Is this granola bar with "whole grains" on the label actually whole food?
- What does "partially hydrogenated" mean for my body?
- Is this yogurt with 15 ingredients better or worse than the one with 5?
- The front of the package says "healthy" — but the ingredient list tells a different story

**Fuel Good's scanner solves this instantly.** Three modes cover every scenario a user encounters:

### 1. Barcode Scan
Point your camera at any barcode. Instant lookup returns the Fuel Score, ingredient flags (seed oils, refined flour, added sugars, preservatives), and a clear verdict: whole food or not. No label reading required.

### 2. Ingredient Label Scan
Take a photo of the ingredient list on any package. The AI reads every ingredient, classifies each one (whole vs processed), flags the problematic ones, and explains *why* in plain language. Users learn what to look for — not just what to avoid.

### 3. Meal Photo Scan
Snap a photo of any prepared food — restaurant meals, home-cooked dishes, cafeteria plates, street food. The AI identifies the dish, estimates ingredients, and scores it. Works for situations where there's no label to read.

### Why This Matters for the System

The scanner is the **entry point to the Fuel Good loop** — it's where users first learn what "whole food" actually means in practice. They scan a bag of chips (Fuel 15), then scan an avocado (Fuel 100), and the difference clicks instantly. That moment of clarity is what converts someone from "I should eat better" to "I know exactly how."

It also creates a **pre-purchase decision loop** that shapes behavior before eating even happens:

```
Grocery store → Scan item → See Fuel Score → Make better choice
→ Bring home better ingredients → Cook better meals
→ Higher weekly average → Earn more flex meals
```

The app isn't just tracking what you already ate — it's shaping what you buy in the first place. Over time, users internalize what to look for: shorter ingredient lists, recognizable ingredients, no seed oils, no refined flour. The scanner educates without lecturing.

**Every scan also shows:**
- Fuel Score with tier badge
- Ingredient-by-ingredient breakdown (flagged: seed oils, refined flour, added sugars, preservatives, emulsifiers, artificial additives)
- Suggested whole-food swaps ("Try X instead — Fuel Score: 95")
- MES estimate (how this food impacts energy levels)
- Whether it counts as a flex meal or a clean meal in the weekly budget

## What Fuel Good Does

### Curated Whole-Food Meals (Fuel Score: 100)
A growing library of chef-quality recipes designed to be nutritious *and* delicious. Every meal is built with whole food ingredients — no seed oils, no refined flour, no artificial anything. Full meals, meal-prep components, and smart pairings. Every single recipe in the app scores Fuel 100 by design.

### Weekly Meal Plans
One-tap meal plan generation tailored to your preferences, dietary needs, and goals. Eliminates decision fatigue — the #1 reason people default to takeout. Includes prep timelines, grocery lists, and quality projections for the week ahead.

Following a Fuel Good meal plan guarantees a high weekly Fuel Score. The plan does the work — users just cook and eat.

### Food Tracking
- **Log from your plan** — tap to log meals you've already planned, guaranteed Fuel 100
- **Scan anything** — barcode, ingredient label, or meal photo for instant Fuel Score + MES analysis
- **Chronometer view** — full daily nutrition breakdown with macro and micronutrient tracking

### Healthify (AI Recipe Transformation)
Craving something unhealthy? Don't fight it — transform it. Tell the AI what you're craving and it generates a whole-food version that satisfies the craving while keeping your Fuel Score at 100. Mac and cheese becomes a high-protein baked pasta. A milkshake becomes a protein-packed smoothie. The craving is honored, the ingredients are upgraded.

### Gamification
Daily quests, XP, achievements, and streaks that make consistent healthy eating feel like progression, not obligation. You're not just eating — you're leveling up. Every quest completed, every streak maintained, every achievement unlocked reinforces the habit loop that makes clean eating automatic.

## Strategic Vision: Where We're Going

### Making the Reward Loop Visible

The flex system should be emotionally present at every touchpoint:

- **Every meal logged** → user sees how it impacts their flex progress
- **Every flex meal earned** → celebration with visual feedback
- **Today's plan** → shows what each meal contributes to the reward
- **Weekly summary** → proves the philosophy with real numbers

### Homepage Psychology

The homepage should answer three questions in this order:
1. **"What have I earned?"** — Flex progress, weekly score, rewards unlocked
2. **"What should I eat next?"** — Today's plan with meals that look appetizing
3. **"How am I doing?"** — Scores, streaks, quests (supporting data, not hero data)

The reward comes first. The action comes second. The metrics come third.

### Scanner as Education Layer

The scanner is more than a tracking tool — it's how users learn to see food differently:

- **Instant clarity at the grocery store** — no more guessing, no more misleading labels
- **Learn by doing** — every scan teaches users what to look for (short ingredient lists, recognizable ingredients, no seed oils)
- **Pre-purchase decisions** — the app shapes what users buy, not just what they log after eating
- **Swap suggestions** — when a product scores low, show a better alternative ("Try X instead — Fuel 95")
- **Over time, users internalize the knowledge** — they start recognizing whole food vs processed without needing the scanner. The app has done its job

The scanner turns Fuel Good from a logging app into a **nutrition literacy tool** that meets users where they are — in the grocery aisle, at a restaurant, staring at a menu — and gives them clarity in real-time.

### Meal Appeal

Recipes should make users *want* to cook. This means:
- Visual appetite appeal (food photography or rich descriptions)
- Fuel Score prominently displayed ("Fuel 100 — pure whole food")
- Connection to the reward: "This meal earns flex progress"
- Taste-first positioning: lead with flavor, not nutrition data

### Celebration Design

Earning flex meals should feel rewarding:
- Visual celebration when a flex meal is earned (glow, animation, satisfying feedback)
- Congratulatory copy: "You earned this! Enjoy it guilt-free"
- Weekly milestones: "Target crushed — 6 flex meals earned this week"
- The dopamine of progress should rival the dopamine of the food itself

## Getting Started

### Prerequisites
- Node.js 20+
- Python 3.11+
- PostgreSQL 15+ with pgvector extension

### Backend

```bash
cd backend
docker-compose up -d              # PostgreSQL + pgvector
cp .env.example .env              # Configure API keys & database URL
pip install -r requirements.txt
alembic upgrade head              # Run migrations
uvicorn app.main:app --reload     # Start server
```

### Frontend

```bash
cd frontend
npm install
npx expo start                    # Start Expo dev server
```

For social authentication (Google/Apple), see [OAUTH_SETUP.md](OAUTH_SETUP.md).

### Key Environment Variables

| Variable | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| `DATABASE_URL` | PostgreSQL connection string |
