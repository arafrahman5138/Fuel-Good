# Whole Foods Guide

A guide to real, nutrient-dense whole foods that support optimal health.

## Core Whole Foods

### Animal Proteins
- Meat (beef, bison, venison)
- Wild fish
- Chicken
- Turkey
- Elk
- Lamb
- Organ meats (liver, heart, kidney - the most nutrient-dense foods available)
- Shellfish (oysters, mussels, shrimp, crab, clams - rich in zinc, B12, selenium)
- Dairy (milk, cheese, yogurt, kefir)
- Eggs
- Whey
- Bone broth (collagen, glycine, minerals)

### Whole Food Fats
- Avocados and olives
- Coconut (oil, milk, flesh)
- Butter and ghee
- Tallow and lard

### Nuts & Seeds
- Almonds, walnuts, macadamias, pecans, cashews
- Chia seeds, flax seeds, hemp seeds, pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds

### Carbohydrate Sources
- Fruit (all varieties)
- Potatoes (white and sweet)
- Starches (root vegetables, squash)
- Cooked vegetables
- Fermented vegetables (sauerkraut, kimchi, pickles)
- Rice (white and brown)
- Properly prepared legumes and grains (if tolerated)

### Natural Sweeteners
- Dark chocolate (high cacao %)
- Honey (raw preferred)
- Maple syrup (pure)
- Fruit juices (in moderation)

### Herbs, Spices & Sea Vegetables
- Herbs and spices (turmeric, ginger, garlic, rosemary, cinnamon)
- Sea vegetables (nori, kelp, dulse - iodine and trace minerals)

## Principles

1. **Prioritize nutrient-dense protein** - Animal proteins provide the most bioavailable nutrients
2. **Take targeted supplements if needed** - Fill gaps based on individual needs (Vitamin D, magnesium, omega-3s are common)
3. **Find what works for you** - Personalize based on tolerance, goals, and how you feel
4. **Properly prepare grains and legumes** - Soaking, sprouting, or fermenting improves digestibility
5. **Moderation with sweeteners** - Even natural sweeteners should be consumed mindfully
